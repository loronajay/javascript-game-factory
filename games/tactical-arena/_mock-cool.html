    Field Manual

    • Each player activates every living piece once per squad turn. Last squad standing wins.
    • A piece may move then act, or act then move — but a move-alone activation is not allowed. Every activation must end with an attack, ART, or Defend.
    • An ART replaces the whole activation and cannot be used after moving or attacking. ARTS spend MP; MP never auto-recovers.
    • Physical damage = max(1, STR − DEF). Magic ignores DEF. True damage bypasses both DEF and Defend.
    • Attack rolls: 10% miss, 15% critical (×1.5 damage). Blind units always miss. Attacking ARTS roll twice — first to hit, then for their status or heal effect. A missed swing lands no effect.
    • Defend halves all incoming physical and magic damage (rounded up) until the unit next activates.
    • RAGE triggers automatically at 5 HP or lower. Read each unit's Codex entry for its specific bonus.
    • Volley Shot and Footwork deal true damage and never roll to hit.
    • Kill before spreading. A dead unit can't act — focus fire one target completely rather than chipping multiple enemies.
    • Time your debuffs. Status durations vary by ability — check each ability's description. The current Blind and Silence abilities each last 1 turn, so cast them right before the enemy's activation for maximum disruption.
    • Poison is permanent. Unlike Blind and Silence, Poison never fades. Stack it on high-HP targets who will take many activation-start ticks before they fall.
    • Respect RAGE. Units at 5 HP or below become more dangerous. Finish low-HP targets rather than leaving them to activate with RAGE bonuses.
    • Defend is timing-based. It halves all hits until the unit next acts. Use it when multiple enemies will attack before your next squad turn.
    • Footwork is a traversal tool. It passes through enemies for 2 true damage each tile — use it to reach a backline target and chip the frontline in the same activation.
    • MP is scarce. ARTS never recover MP. Save it for activations where it changes the outcome, not just any time you can afford the cost.
    • Volley Shot punishes clusters. Aim the cone to hit multiple enemies. Close Shot bonuses still apply per target — the Archer can hit harder up close than expected.
