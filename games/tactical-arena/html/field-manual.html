Field Manual

  • Each player activates every living piece once per squad turn. Last squad standing wins.
  • An activation is move then act or act then move — but a move-alone activation is not allowed. Every activation ends with an attack, ART, or Defend.
  • An ART replaces the whole activation and cannot follow a move or attack. ARTS spend MP (or an alternate resource); MP never auto-recovers.
  • Physical damage = max(1, STR − DEF). Magic ignores DEF. True damage bypasses both DEF and Defend.
  • Attacks roll 7% miss, 15% critical (×1.5 damage). Attacking ARTS roll first to hit, then separately for their status or heal effect — a missed swing lands no effect.
  • Defend halves incoming physical and magic damage (rounded up) until the unit next activates.
  • RAGE triggers automatically at 5 HP or lower. Read each unit's Codex entry for its specific bonus.

New here? These seven rules are enough to play a legal turn. The Systems tab explains statuses, summons, auras, and other board mechanics; the Codex lists every unit's exact kit.

  • Status effects (debuffs). Five kinds — blind (next attack misses), silence (no ARTS), poison (damage at each activation start, usually permanent until cleansed), slow (reduced Move), and stun (auto-spent, can't be selected). Durations vary per ability — check the Codex — and some units are immune to specific statuses.
  • Buffs. Many ARTS and passives grant temporary boosts instead — extra STR, DEF, Move, or range on an ally. Like debuffs, buffs run on a duration and then expire.
  • True damage. A growing set of abilities deal damage that ignores both DEF and Defend. It's the answer to high-armor walls and permanent-defend plans — when a target hides behind armor, reach for a true-damage option.
  • Auras. Some units carry an always-on zone that buffs nearby allies or debuffs nearby enemies. Its reach shows as a tinted area on the board, so positioning in or out of it matters as much as targeting.
  • Battlefield objects. Certain units place objects on tiles: destructible walls block movement and line of sight until knocked down, and fire burns any unit standing on it at each turn rollover.
  • Summoned units. Summons (like the Necromancer's Ghoul) occupy a tile and can be killed, but never take their own turn and don't count toward victory — defeat is decided by living squad commanders only.
  • Non-combatant commanders. A few pieces (like the King) never attack and must be given a command before the rest of their squad can act. Losing one alone doesn't end the match unless it's the last piece standing.
  • Weather. Some casters change the whole board's weather — blizzard, spring shower, heatwave, or thunderstorm — each shifting something (range, restoration, crits, or ART costs) for everyone until it changes.
  • Board polarity. The board is a checkerboard of light and dark tiles. A handful of units gain effects from the tile they stand on, so where a piece sits can be part of its kit.
  • Kill before spreading. A dead unit can't act — focus fire one target completely rather than chipping several enemies.
  • Time your disables. Short-lived debuffs — blind, silence, slow, stun — do the most work when cast right before the enemy would act. Deny the activation you fear most, not the first one you can reach.
  • Poison rewards patience. Because poison usually never fades, stack it on high-HP targets who will eat many activation-start ticks before they fall.
  • Respect RAGE. Units at 5 HP or below become more dangerous. Finish low-HP targets rather than leaving them to activate with a RAGE bonus.
  • Defend against the crowd. Defend halves every hit until the unit next acts — raise it when several enemies will swing before your next squad turn, not by reflex.
  • Spend MP on swings, not fillers. ARTS never refund MP. Save it for the activation that changes the outcome, not any time you can afford the cost.
  • True damage answers armor. When a high-DEF body or a permanent-defend plan stonewalls you, switch to an ability that ignores DEF and Defend outright.
  • Auras reward standing still. A buff or debuff aura works just by existing — hold formation inside your own zone, and pull units out of the enemy's.
  • Sequence your commander. If your side has a non-combatant commander, its command is spent first each turn — plan that command around what the squad needs, not by habit.
  • Walls buy time, not kills. Use destructible cover to stall a lane or shield a low-HP ally for a turn, and expect it to come down.
  • Read the whole board. Weather, tile polarity, summons, and auras change a fight's math. Check what's active before committing a plan — the roster brings far more than raw damage rolls, and each unit's full kit is in the Codex.
  • Count the support turn. Age, Time Stretch, Anoint, heals, and commands usually spend the caster's whole activation. They are strongest when they create an immediate reach break, deny an enemy turn, or make one ally's next several activations worth more than the support turn you spent. The Witch Doctor's dances are the exception: each is a bonus action, so he can dance and still move and attack the same turn.
  • Try Father Time with Sniper or Angel as a super carry. Age can stack +STR or +DEF on the same ally and lasts until Father Time falls. Sniper converts that STR into long-range, wall-and-body-piercing shots; Angel converts it into DEF-ignoring magic basics while staying status-immune. Time Stretch is the tempo button when +1 MOVE creates the shot this turn.
  • Try cover around the carry plan. Sniper's Build Cover blocks movement and line of sight for most pieces, but Sniper still shoots through it. That makes cover more than defense: it can buy the extra turns Father Time needs to Age the carry, then let the carry keep firing through the barricade.
  • Try magic next to magic support. Nemesis makes allied magic hit +1 harder and trims MP costs while alive. Magician, Fat Wizard, Angel basics, Necromancer, Virus, and Gargoyle's Pyroclasm all care because magic ignores DEF; Defend, silence, and magic reduction are the things that answer it.
  • Try status chains before the damage payoff. Virus spreads debuffs from an afflicted enemy to nearby enemy allies, Witch Doctor's Misfortune Stance doubles status chances for everyone, and Archer, Swordsman, Mystic, Necromancer, Sniper, Fat Wizard, and Virus all bring statuses. The setup turn is worth it when poison enables board-wide true damage or blind/silence denies key enemy activations.
  • Try sustain around a real anchor. Mystic's Guardian gives the team +1 DEF, Paladin heals nearby allies when his physical hits land, and Fat Cleric or Fat Wizard can patch damage directly. Put those tools near a tank like Gargoyle, Clod, Juggernaut, or Fat Knight when you want the fight to take longer, but keep a damage plan so healing turns do not just stall.
  • Try true damage when armor is the problem. Footwork, Volley Shot, Time Steal, Fart, Flight, Poison Tick, Self Destruct, Explosion, Thunderous Charge, and several light-tile effects ignore DEF and Defend. They are the pressure valves against high-DEF walls and permanent defending plans.
  • Try Clod as a formation piece. Brick House gives adjacent allies +1 DEF and gives Clod +1 STR per sheltered ally, while Rock Hard blanks physical damage when he defends. The reward is real, but it asks you to keep pieces close enough to benefit without donating an easy area target.
  • Try King when you want timing puzzles. Commands are global for the turn and scale with allied RAGE: Strike for damage, Hold for DEF and healing, Pursue for MOVE, Higher Ground for range. He must act first and bleeds when allies fall, so the fun is sequencing, not autopilot power.
  • Try the fat family for the designed combo. Fat Knight, Fat Wizard, Fat Cleric, and Fat Bowman each unlock a small Brothers in Arms bonus when the other three are alive. You get four 30-HP bodies with healing, splash magic, ranged scaling, and frontline shove tools instead of one single trick.
  • Try immunity as part of the plan. Paladin, Angel, Gargoyle, and King ignore every status, Mystic and Nemesis ignore silence, Father Time ignores stun and slow, and cleanses from Mystic or Fat Cleric can reset a bad turn. A synergy that folds to blind, poison, or silence needs one of those answers nearby.
