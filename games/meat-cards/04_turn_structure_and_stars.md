# Turn Structure and Star Economy

## Stars Per Turn

Each player has 5 stars to use on their turn.

Players must use all 5 stars each turn.

If a player ends their turn without using all 5 stars, they take damage equal to each unused star.

Before ending their turn, a player may discard 1 card per unused star to avoid that star penalty damage.

This discard-to-cover-unused-stars action must be the final voluntary action of the turn.

## Star Spending Examples

If a player spends 3 stars and has 2 unused stars:

- They would take 2 player damage at end of turn.
- Or they may discard 2 cards as the final voluntary action to avoid the 2 damage.

If a player spends all 5 stars:

- No star penalty damage is taken.

## Delayed Star Costs

Some interrupt/reaction-like effects create a delayed star cost.

Delayed star costs:

- Stack.
- Are automatically applied at the start of that player's next turn.
- Count as stars already spent for that turn.
- Cannot be refused.
- Under the current card pool, cannot exceed 5 total owed stars.

Example:

A player triggers a 2-star reaction during the opponent's turn.

At the start of that player's next turn:

- 2 stars are already spent.
- 3 stars remain available.
- If they spend the remaining 3, they have used all 5 and take no star penalty.

## Turn Flow

Current turn flow:

1. Start turn.
2. Reset that player's monster attack and ability uses.
3. Apply delayed star costs owed from prior effects.
4. Draw turn card(s).
5. Main action phase:
   - Summon monsters.
   - Equip accessories.
   - Use monster abilities.
   - Attack with monsters.
   - Play Later cards.
6. Optional final discard to cover unused stars.
7. Discard down to 7 cards if needed.
8. Pass turn.

## First-Turn Restrictions

Each player's first turn is setup-only.

Offensive actions are not allowed during setup turns.

Offensive action means any action or effect that affects the opponent, their monsters, their cards, their resources, their stats, their board, or their available actions in any way.
